A study library on the Blessed Virgin Mary as Mediatrix and Co-Redemptrix, drawn from Genesis 3:15 through twenty centuries of patristic, medieval, and magisterial witness.

The three pillars

Foundation

The doctrine rests on the unique mediation of Christ (1 Tim 2:5) and Mary’s subordinate cooperation within it: never displacing it, never parallel to it.

in Christo et sub Christo

Testimony

From Irenaeus through the Akathist Hymn, Bernard of Clairvaux, Bonaventure, and the modern Magisterium: the same doctrine in Greek, Latin, and the Church’s own dogmatic acts.

vox traditionis

Devotion

The rosary, the Litany of Loreto, the Office of Readings, the Akathist, the great apparitions: the lived prayer that the doctrine produces.

The Other Eleven Sections

II OT Types Twenty-eight Old-Testament figures in which the Church has read the Mother of God: Eve, the Ark, the burning bush, the Gebirah, Judith, Esther. III NT Texts The three load-bearing passages, Cana, Calvary, and Revelation 12, in Nestle-Aland Greek, with multi-point exegesis. IV Anthology One sentence per saint, fifty-six voices: Irenaeus, Ephrem, Augustine, Bernard, Bonaventure, Aquinas, Newman. Greek and Latin originals beside the English. V Rosary Twenty mysteries on the Dominican distribution. Each with Scripture, patristic anchor, meditation, fruit, intention. VI Defense Twelve Protestant objections steel-manned, four-layer Catholic response. Luther, Calvin, Zwingli, Wesley in their own words defending perpetual virginity. VII Feasts Eighteen Marian feasts of the liturgical year: date, rank, readings, Collect in Latin for principals, suggested devotion. VIII Apparitions Seven primary canonical apparitions: Guadalupe, Rue du Bac, La Salette, Lourdes, Knock, Fatima, Akita. With the 1978 CDF norms of discernment. IX Litany of Loreto Fifty-four titles in six structural groups. Latin and English, scriptural root per title, history of papal additions from Sixtus V (1587) to Francis (2020). X Office of Readings Patristic Office texts for fourteen Marian feasts: Athanasius, Leo, Bede, Augustine's cooperata est, John Damascene, Andrew of Crete, Bernard. XI Akathist The complete sixth-century Byzantine Marian hymn, the famous chairetismoi in Greek, with per-stanza annotation. XII Iconography Byzantine and Western iconographic types, symbolic vocabulary decoded, famous miraculous images by region, the iconography of each Marian dogma.
